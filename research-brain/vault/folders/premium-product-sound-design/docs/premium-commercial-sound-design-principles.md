# Premium Commercial Sound Design Principles

Professional commercial sound design is not the act of attaching a sound to every movement. It is the controlled design of realism, weight, timing, space, and brand perception.

The house rule for premium AI-assisted product advertising:

> Sound must always be motivated by visible action, believable space, or deliberate brand intent.

If a sound does not improve realism, weight, continuity, emotion, or brand recognition, it usually does not belong in the cut.

## What Makes Sound Feel Expensive

Premium audio is built from a small number of clearly defined layers:

- believable ambience
- correctly chosen material sounds
- selective motion accents
- restrained transitions
- a balanced final mix where music, effects, and speech do not fight each other

The premium feeling comes from selection, timing, hierarchy, and plausibility. Loudness and complexity do not make a track feel expensive by themselves.

## Why Foley Matters

Foley and sound effects exist to make viewers believe what they see. They provide everyday tactile detail that convinces the brain an object is present.

When foley is mismatched, too loud, too generic, too continuous, or physically unmotivated, the result quickly feels synthetic, amateur, or AI-generated.

Good foley can make a product feel:

- heavier
- cleaner
- warmer
- more precise
- more luxurious
- more physically present

## Studio-Grade vs Weak AI Sound

Amateur sound usually shows up as:

- repeated library swooshes
- material sounds that do not match the product
- music covering the tactile details
- loud transitions used to create false drama
- a sound for every keyframe

Weak AI sound often adds:

- generic whooshes
- inconsistent ambience
- arbitrary impacts
- decorative sparkle with no physical basis
- random changes in sonic perspective between shots

Studio-grade sound:

- supports visible motion
- preserves product scale
- lets the viewer notice the product rather than the trick
- stays restrained enough to feel intentional
- uses contrast and dynamics instead of constant stimulation

## Silence Is a Premium Tool

Silence is part of sound design. In product reels, the absence of sound before a cap click, box open, watch settle, or logo sting can make the event feel more deliberate and expensive.

Overfilled tracks create the opposite impression: nervous, noisy, try-hard commercials that feel cheap. Strong mixes rely on contrast and dynamics rather than constant audio decoration.

## Five Functional Sound Families

Use five functional families instead of an unsorted sound library.

| Family | Includes | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Reality layers | room tone, ambience, air, environmental hiss, handling, cloth, surface contact | Make the shot believable |
| Motion layers | soft whooshes, pass-bys, low air movement, servo-like glides, micro-swells | Support camera or object movement without cartoon exaggeration |
| Material layers | paper grain, cardboard friction, glass resonance, metallic click, plastic tap, leather creak, liquid movement | Tell the viewer what the object is made of |
| Structural layers | impacts, settle sounds, risers, downlifters, transition tails, stingers | Shape edit rhythm and shot architecture |
| Brand layers | logo sting, sonic mnemonic, UI ticks, confirmation sounds, repeatable branded gestures | Build a coherent sonic identity across campaigns |

The practical rule:

> Use the lowest-intensity layer that still communicates the action clearly.

This protects realism and premium perception better than escalating every moment into a bigger sound.

## Product-Readability Standard

Treat sound design as a product-readability system, not decoration.

Use sound to clarify:

- what material is on screen
- how heavy or delicate the object is
- where the product sits in space
- which moment matters in the edit
- whether the brand feels refined, warm, technical, minimal, or energetic

Avoid sounds that exist only because the edit feels empty. If the space, material, or brand intent does not justify the sound, silence or ambience is usually better.
