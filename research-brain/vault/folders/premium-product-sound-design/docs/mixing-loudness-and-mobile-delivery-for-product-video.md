# Mixing, Loudness, and Mobile Delivery for Product Video

Professional commercial sound is made in the mix, not in the source files alone. AI-generated audio should be treated as raw material that still needs editorial control.

## Core Post-Production Tasks

For a serious product ad, finish the audio with:

- audio tagging or organization into Dialogue, Music, SFX, and Ambience
- ducking when dialogue or voiceover is present
- LUFS and true-peak metering
- reverb for realistic space and depth
- panning when motion justifies it
- noise reduction for hum, hiss, or unwanted background elements
- manual automation for key product moments
- final loudness control

## Layering Standard

A strong product-video layering workflow usually consists of:

1. one ambience bed
2. one movement bed
3. one material layer
4. one impact or settle layer where needed
5. optional micro-detail only when it adds something unique

Real soundscapes are layered, but the job is careful blending, not accumulation. Small product actions usually need fewer layers than cinematic transitions because their believability depends more on surface truth than magnitude.

## Mix Tools and Their Purpose

| Tool | Use in premium product audio |
|---|---|
| EQ | Create space between music, foley, ambience, and voiceover |
| Compression | Smooth dynamics and help sounds sit in the mix, while staying transparent |
| Automation | Shape emphasis more elegantly than heavy compression |
| Reverb | Place the object in a believable space |
| Panning | Support visible movement when the picture justifies it |
| Ducking | Protect speech, CTA moments, and important product foley |
| Noise reduction | Remove distractions that make a commercial feel unfinished |

Processing should serve clarity rather than become a signature effect.

## Loudness Reference

The practical loudness standard:

> Measure integrated loudness, watch short-term peaks, and leave true-peak headroom.

The source identifies ITU-R BS.1770-5 as the measurement basis for programme loudness and true-peak level. It also notes that EBU-style short-form commercial delivery specs commonly use approximately:

- **-23 LUFS programme loudness**
- **maximum short-term +5 LU / around -18 LUFS**
- **maximum true peak -1 dBTP**
- an EBU-mode loudness meter

Those values are more relevant to TV-style spots than most social exports, but they are useful calibration references for disciplined short-form mixing.

## Social Platform Mindset

For social platforms, do not mix like a cinema trailer.

The source highlights that YouTube re-encodes uploads and recommends high-quality source uploads. It also notes that YouTube playback tools such as Stable volume and Voice boost can reduce loudness variation and enhance speech intelligibility. TikTok ad guidance requires sound or music, 9:16 orientation, safe-zone visibility, and clear audio.

For social mixes:

- control low-end
- avoid harsh upper mids
- keep transient peaks civilized
- ensure key tactile details remain audible on small speakers
- protect speech and CTA moments
- export a high-quality source because platforms re-encode

## Mobile-First Mixing Rule

For vertical reels:

- Keep ambience and music clean enough that the first 1-2 seconds remain intelligible on a phone speaker.
- Avoid excessive sub energy. Size can be suggested with mid and low-mid design rather than inaudible bass.
- Protect speech and CTA moments through ducking and automation, not blunt global limiting.
- Check the final export with loudness and true-peak metering rather than by ear alone.
- Listen on phone speakers as well as studio monitors.

## Music and SFX Mix Checklist

- Is music supporting the product rather than masking it?
- Is there enough EQ separation between the bed and the foley?
- Are compression choices transparent rather than obviously squashy?
- Are reverb tails matched across cuts?
- Are stereo movements motivated by the image?

## Voiceover Clarity Checklist

- Have dialogue, music, SFX, and ambience been separated by function?
- Has music or ambience been ducked behind speech?
- Has harshness around speech intelligibility been controlled?
- Is the CTA frame the clearest spoken or textual moment in the mix?

## Social Export Checklist

- Export a high-quality source because distribution platforms re-encode.
- Check 9:16 framing and safe-zone compatibility alongside clear audio.
- Monitor integrated loudness, short-term peaks, and true peak before export.
- Listen on phone speakers as well as studio monitors.
