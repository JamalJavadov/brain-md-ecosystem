# Product Video Sound Recipes, Templates, and Checklists

These recipes translate the standards into production-ready sound direction for product commercials. They are intended to be adapted per product, shot length, and brand tone.

## Premium Package Hero Reveal

| Field | Direction |
|---|---|
| Scene mood | Calm authority, premium reveal |
| Visual movement | Slow push-in or slight orbit, final settle |
| Base ambience | Quiet premium studio air |
| Main SFX | Low restrained motion bed, soft contact on settle |
| Foley | Light cardboard or coated-box handling texture if the object visibly moves |
| Transition sound | Minimal, often unnecessary |
| Music relationship | Music can lead, but foley owns the final settle |
| Mixing style | Low-noise, wide headroom, short tails |

Prompt wording:

```md
subtle premium studio ambience, soft low whoosh matching the camera push-in, tactile premium box presence, soft contact sound as the package settles
```

Negative wording:

```md
no random whooshes, no oversized impacts, no fake cinematic boom
```

## Copy-Ready Prompt Templates

```md
Elegant luxury perfume bottle on a dark premium background.
Slow, controlled rotation and cap reveal.
Audio:
Near-silent luxury ambience.
Quiet glass resonance as the bottle turns.
Precise premium cap click.
Subtle liquid motion inside the bottle.
Music remains soft and secondary to the tactile details.
Negative: fake magical sparkle, oversized glass hits, booming impacts, random whooshes.
```

```md
Macro close-up of a premium watch.
Slow push-in across the dial, crown detail, then clasp close.
Audio:
Intimate micro-mechanical detail.
Precise metallic ticks and tight brushed metal friction.
Short premium clasp click.
Very subtle air movement only during camera motion.
Negative: giant impacts, sci-fi beeps, exaggerated ticking.
```

### Printing Agency or Flyer Ad Sound Prompt

```md
A premium printing reel showing flyers, folded brochures, and booklet spreads.
Audio:
Soft tactile paper slides, gentle premium page turns, neat stack alignment taps.
Subtle clean room tone.
Short editorial transition accents only on visible cut energy.
Music stays below paper foley.
Negative: cheap crinkly paper, cartoonish flips, harsh highs, random whooshes.
```

### Coffee Product Sound Prompt

```md
Warm coffee package commercial with cafe atmosphere.
Audio:
Warm cafe ambience, subtle steam, natural coffee bean texture, soft paper bag opening, elegant cup placement.
Transitions are short and warm.
Negative: harsh hiss, oversized pours, crunchy plastic tears, excessive boom.
```

### Negative Audio Prompt Template

```md
random whooshes, excessive cinematic booms, artificial sparkle, material mismatch, loud impacts for small objects, inconsistent ambience, harsh over-compression, cluttered mix, music masking foley, speech masking, fake neon UI sounds
```

In tools that support a dedicated negative prompt field, exclusions should go there instead of being embedded as raw "no" or "do not" language in the main shot description.

## Pre-Production Sound Checklist

- Define the role of sound in each shot: tactile realism, motion support, transition support, brand cue, or voiceover support.
- Identify the true material of every visible object before sourcing or prompting SFX.
- Decide in advance which moments deserve silence.
- Choose whether the soundtrack is music-led, foley-led, or VO-led.
- For AI generation, separate visual prompt logic from audio prompt logic.

## Product Material Sound Checklist

- Paper: soft fiber, page, fold, and slide sounds; never plastic crackle.
- Kraft: warm cardboard friction and soft fold movement.
- Glass: quiet resonance, precise clicks, restrained liquid details.
- Metal: engineered precision, not loudness.
- Plastic: soft controlled clicks, not toy-like thin taps.
- Fabric or leather: friction and micro-texture, kept restrained.
- Coffee: warm context plus tactile product sounds.

## Motion Synchronization Checklist

- Is there sound only where the eye expects a physical consequence?
- Do anticipation, contact, settle, and decay read clearly?
- Are camera movement, object movement, and sound movement proportionate rather than competing?
- Are motion accents shorter on mobile-first edits?

## Final Quality-Control Checklist

- Does every sound match a visible action, space, or deliberate brand cue?
- Does the product sound match its material?
- Is the sound realistic for the object size?
- Are whooshes used only when needed?
- Is ambience continuous and believable across cuts?
- Are impacts too loud, too low-heavy, or too long for the shot?
- Is music fighting the SFX or voiceover?
- Is the voiceover clear?
- Does the video sound premium on mobile speakers?
- Does anything in the soundtrack feel random, generic, overdone, or AI-made?

## Final House Rules

- Prompt audio deliberately in separate sentences.
- Generate in single-scene chunks when short-form coherence matters.
- Anchor image-to-video shots with a strong first frame and prompt motion-only consequences.
- Use material-accurate foley more often than generic cinematic FX.
- Let music support, not dominate, product tactility.
- Finish every serious job with metering, automation, cleanup, and manual editorial judgement.
