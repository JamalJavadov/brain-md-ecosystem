# ARTIZ.AZ Professional Visual Standard and QC

A publishable ARTIZ.AZ asset should pass three gates:

1. **Brand coherence**
2. **Physical credibility**
3. **Commercial clarity**

If a frame is stylish but fails any one of those gates, clients are more likely to read it as AI art than as studio work.

## Professional Checklist

Use this checklist before approving any still, reel, or motion test.

| Check | Pass criteria |
|---|---|
| **Concept check** | The visual can be summarised in one sentence without using style words such as "cool", "modern", or "luxury". |
| **Brand consistency check** | It clearly belongs to ARTIZ.AZ's material, colour, and motion system rather than to a generic AI aesthetic. |
| **Material realism check** | All hero surfaces show believable roughness, edge treatment, thickness, and reflection behaviour. |
| **Lighting check** | Every highlight and shadow can be explained by a plausible light source or reflective environment. |
| **Typography check** | All final text is manually controlled, aligned to a grid, contrast-checked, and written in a deliberate language system. |
| **Composition check** | There is one dominant read, one secondary read, and no unnecessary decorative clutter. |
| **Camera and motion check** | The camera reveals information instead of moving only because motion feels impressive. |
| **Physics check** | Anything that floats, rotates, shifts, or assembles has believable weight, inertia, and contact behaviour. |
| **Commercial value check** | The visual communicates a service or product benefit rather than only communicating style. |
| **AI-likeness check** | If the logo is removed, a stranger would still read the frame as a real campaign image rather than a prompt result. |

## Scoring Rubric

Score each asset from 1 to 10.

| Score | Read by viewers as | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **1-2** | Obviously AI-generated | Generic styling, bad text, weak structure, random motion, and visual cliches dominate. |
| **3-4** | AI-assisted amateur | Some attractive surfaces, but composition, type, and motion still feel templated or arbitrary. |
| **5-6** | Semi-professional | Decent craft, but the asset lacks a strong brand system or product logic; it may work as social content, not as portfolio-defining work. |
| **7-8** | Professional studio-level | Controlled materials, deliberate camera, readable type, coherent brand language, and strong commercial legibility. |
| **9-10** | Premium agency-level | Distinct visual language, near-flawless execution, clear product or service storytelling, scalable system thinking, and immediate brand ownership. |

## ARTIZ.AZ Visual System

| System area | ARTIZ.AZ standard |
|---|---|
| **Colour direction** | Use a restrained palette built around charcoal or graphite, warm copper or ember orange, and stone or bone neutrals. Avoid beige AI-luxury gradients as a default. Gradients should emerge from light falloff, not wallpaper-like backgrounds. |
| **Material direction** | Primary materials: brushed or anodised metal, smoked or frosted acrylic/glass, matte ceramic, and when relevant to print clients, coated and uncoated paper stock with visible grain. No frame should use more than two hero materials plus one accent finish. |
| **Lighting direction** | Prefer motivated studio lighting that is soft but directional. Use large soft sources to shape edges, then add controlled rim light only when it helps form or separation. Avoid over-bloom, omnidirectional glow, and fake holographic premium wash. |
| **Logo usage rules** | Lock one master 2D logo and one master 3D build. Do not let AI redraw, melt, stretch, or invent typographic variants. Animate around the logo with camera, light, sectional reveals, reframing, or subtle assembly language. |
| **Composition rules** | Each shot should contain one hero object, one support layer, and one information layer at most. If more than three elements compete for attention, reduce. Strong negative space is a premium signal; clutter is not. |
| **Motion rules** | ARTIZ.AZ motion should feel patient, tactile, and precise. Prioritise glides, reveals, rotations with weight, assembly motions, sliding mechanisms, and controlled camera moves. Ban perpetual drift, random orbiting, and shapeless morphs. |
| **Typography rules** | Use a fixed pair or superfamily with clear hierarchy. Keep service labels short. Final type should be composited manually in post whenever possible. Use one language per asset unless a bilingual treatment is explicitly part of the concept. |
| **Reel style rules** | For 9:16 reels, structure most assets as hook, proof, and sign-off. Open with an unmistakable category cue inside the first second. Keep all critical content centre-safe. Motion may be quick, but not chaotic. |
| **Anti-cliche rules** | Ban sparkles, decorative stars, arbitrary glowing panels, meaningless chrome, unreadable text-on-glass, fake lens dirt, and "luxury background" prompts unless specifically justified by the concept. |
| **Pipeline rules** | Recommended workflow: concept sentence, reference board, material board, typography frame, style frame, animatic, AI generation or simulation, manual typography and compositing, QC score. Premium results come from this order, not from skipping ahead to final generation. |

## Approval Gates

### Gate 1: Brand Coherence

The asset should feel owned by ARTIZ.AZ before the logo appears.

Check:

- restrained colour palette;
- recurring material families;
- consistent typography;
- disciplined negative space;
- recognizable motion cadence;
- no generic luxury defaults.

### Gate 2: Physical Credibility

The image should behave like a designed world.

Check:

- material roughness is not uniform;
- hard edges have believable bevels or thickness;
- contact shadows ground objects;
- reflections have plausible sources;
- floating elements have a reason;
- depth of field does not hide weak composition;
- text in-world has perspective, occlusion, spacing, and surface logic.

### Gate 3: Commercial Clarity

The visual should sell a service or product benefit, not only a mood.

Check:

- the first second communicates the category or offer;
- proof shots show material, lighting, motion, product quality, or service value;
- the ending has a clean brand sign-off;
- the viewer can say what ARTIZ.AZ is offering;
- style does not overpower comprehension.

## One-Sentence Standard

Create fewer elements, with clearer purpose, stronger materials, stricter typography, slower and more intentional motion, and a narrower brand palette. Let AI serve that direction instead of defining it.

Every visual must prove intent. If a client can infer why the material, camera, pacing, and type were chosen, the work will read as professional. If the choices feel merely plausible, decorative, or randomly premium, the work will read as AI-assisted.
