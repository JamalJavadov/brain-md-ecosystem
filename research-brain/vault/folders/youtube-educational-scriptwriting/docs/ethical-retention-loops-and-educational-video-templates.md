# Ethical Retention Loops and Educational Video Templates

Retention loops keep viewers watching by creating questions, expectations, or unresolved stakes that will be answered later.

They must be ethical: every promised payoff should be delivered.

## Ethical Open Loops

Open loops are promises of future information.

Useful forms:

- Mini-cliffhangers
- Curiosity gaps
- Rehooks
- End-of-video or series teasers

The key constraint is no misleading clickbait. Do not promise a payoff the video does not deliver.

## Mini-Cliffhangers

At natural breaks, hint at a valuable moment coming next.

Examples:

> Next, I will reveal the one thing everyone misses.

> Coming up: a surprising counterexample.

Use these only when the next section genuinely contains the promised information.

## Curiosity Gaps

Pose a question without answering it immediately.

Example:

> Which of these three methods works best? We will find out in a moment.

The brain dislikes unanswered questions, so the viewer has a reason to stay until the loop closes.

## Rehooks

Rehooks reset attention when a section becomes predictable.

Examples:

> This sounds simple, but watch what happens when...

> This is where most people get stuck.

> That covers X, but here is another twist.

Use rehooks at transitions, after examples, or before a shift in perspective.

## Teach-Prove-Example-Shift-Rehook

The source cites this mid-video retention pattern:

```markdown
Teach
Prove
Example
Shift
Rehook
```

In practice:

1. Teach the point.
2. Prove why it matters.
3. Show an example.
4. Shift the perspective.
5. Add a rehook that makes the next section feel necessary.

Example:

> That covers X, but here is another twist. This will actually change how you approach Y.

## End-of-Video Teasers

If relevant, tease the next video or later part of a series.

Example:

> Later in this series, I will explore...

Do not use future teasers as a substitute for delivering the current video's promise. The current video should end with the viewer feeling satisfied.

## Explainer Video Template

Purpose: clarify a concept or answer a "what is..." question.

```markdown
Hook:
Present a surprising example or question related to the concept.
Example: "Most people think [myth about topic] is true, but it is not."

Context or Stakes:
Explain why the topic matters now.

Roadmap:
"We will define X, see how it works, and look at an example."

Sections:
Define the concept.
Break it into sub-parts.
Use diagrams and analogies.
Contrast the explanation with common misconceptions.

Recap:
Summarize the main idea in simple terms.

CTA:
Invite the viewer to try an exercise or watch a deeper dive.
```

## Tutorial or How-To Video Template

Purpose: teach a process or skill.

```markdown
Hook:
Pose a problem or desired result.
Example: "Tired of [common pain]? Today we fix it."

Promise:
"By the end, you will be able to [specific outcome]."

Roadmap:
List the steps: "First do X, then Y, finally Z."

Steps:
For each step, demonstrate the action while narrating clearly.
Use screen recording, live demo, callouts, or highlighted pointers.
Provide a simple example.
Mention pitfalls.

Recap:
Reiterate the final outcome and key tips.

CTA:
Suggest the next project or related tutorial.
```

## Beginner Guide Template

Purpose: introduce a topic to viewers with little or no prior knowledge.

Use a tutorial-like structure, but begin more basically.

Include:

- Definitions of core terms
- Very simple examples
- A quick story or analogy early
- Slower pacing
- Assumptions that the viewer has no prior background

Example analogy:

> Learning X is like learning a new language.

## Myth vs Fact Video Template

Purpose: correct common misconceptions.

```markdown
Hook:
"You have probably heard [common myth] about X. But here is why that is wrong."

Format:
Myth
Analysis
Fact

Example:
Myth: You cannot trick the immune system.
Fact: Here is a case where a vaccine works by a clever method.

Wrap-up:
Emphasize the correct understanding and why it matters.
```

Use a consistent structure for each myth so viewers know how the video works.

## List-Based Educational Video Template

Purpose: compare mistakes, strategies, tools, methods, or examples.

```markdown
Hook:
Say why the list matters or is surprising.
Example: "I tried all 7 best [tools or strategies] people recommend. Most were useless."

Criteria or Context:
Explain how items were chosen.
Example: "I am rating these based on..."

Preview:
Tease the most surprising or important item.
Example: "Wait until you see #1. It changed everything."

Items:
For each item, name it, explain why it matters, give an example or demo, and state the verdict.
Use rehooks between items.

Final Ranking or Recap:
Highlight the #1 item or key lessons.

CTA:
Link to a resource or next video.
```

## Problem-Solution Video Template

Purpose: diagnose a problem and teach the solution.

```markdown
Hook:
Demonstrate the problem.
Example: "Have you ever...? It wastes hours."

Agitate:
Show the consequences or frustrations.

Solution Reveal:
Introduce the solution or concept that fixes it.

Demonstration:
Show how to implement the solution step by step.
Use before-and-after examples.

Result:
Show the positive outcome of applying the solution.

CTA:
Encourage trying the solution or subscribing for more tips.
```

## Case Study Video Template

Purpose: teach from a real example or experiment.

```markdown
Hook:
"This channel, company, or experiment looked average. Then one change skyrocketed results."

Setup:
Explain who or what is being studied and the initial baseline.

Turning Point:
Describe the key change, decision, experiment, or strategy.

Pattern:
Show the process or formula that led to the outcome.
Include data or quotes for proof when available.

Outcome:
Present the result, such as improved metrics or a solved problem.
Use charts or testimonials if available.

Lesson:
Generalize what viewers can apply.

CTA:
Prompt viewers to analyze their own situation or try a similar approach.
```

## Template Adaptation Rule

Use these structures as starting points, not rigid scripts. Adapt the language, order, and level of detail to the subject, audience knowledge, and video promise.
