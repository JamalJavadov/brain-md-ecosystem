# Vertical Short-Form Video Safe Zones

As of July 2026, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and TikTok all support full-screen vertical video, but their interface overlays do not cover the same parts of the frame. Treat the measurements below as current working guides, not permanent platform laws.

The safest baseline is a 1080 x 1920, 9:16 canvas with important content kept away from platform UI, captions, call-to-action buttons, action rails, notches, and profile-grid crops.

## Core Rule

For one organic vertical video that needs to survive TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts, use a centered safe zone of about 900 x 1400 px inside the 1080 x 1920 canvas.

```text
1080 x 1920 canvas

Top caution band: ~260 px
Left caution band: ~90 px    SAFE CENTER: 900 x 1400 px    Right caution band: ~90 px
Bottom caution band: ~260 px
```

This is a cross-platform organic template. It is not strict enough for every paid Reels or TikTok ad placement, where lower call-to-action and commerce overlays can consume more of the frame.

## Instagram Reels

Use a 9:16 vertical canvas, typically 1080 x 1920.

For organic Reels, a conservative working range is:

| Frame area | Working caution zone |
|---|---:|
| Top | 150-200 px |
| Bottom | 320-400 px |
| Sides | Leave padding where possible |

Some current safe-zone tools estimate an Instagram-safe center around 1080 x 1320 or 910 x 1386, while another cross-platform guide suggests about 108 px from the top, 320 px from the bottom, and about 60 px of side padding. These differences come from different methods: some define a fully safe rectangle, while others mark the heaviest UI overlay zones.

Paid or sponsored Reels need a stricter bottom margin. The source notes one 2026 Meta-focused guide estimating that Reels can lose about 35% of the frame at the bottom, roughly 670 px, once the full stack of like, comment, share, save, audio, and caption elements is considered.

Practical Reels placement:

- Treat the bottom of the frame as the least forgiving area.
- Keep faces, product labels, CTA copy, price text, and critical branding out of the lower margin.
- Put hook text in the upper-middle.
- Keep persistent branding above the caption stack.
- Place follow, comment, or promo CTAs well above the bottom overlay area.
- Check the Reel cover separately because Reels tab covers may display vertically while the profile grid crops to 4:5.

## YouTube Shorts

Use 1080 x 1920 and 9:16 for vertical Shorts.

Shorts safe-zone guidance clusters around the center of the frame, with special caution for the right-side action rail and bottom information bar.

Current working measurements from the source:

| Frame area | Working caution zone |
|---|---:|
| Top | ~180 px |
| Bottom | ~350 px |
| Left | ~40 px |
| Right | ~120 px |
| Usable area | ~920 x 1390 px |

Another current checker estimates a Shorts safe zone around 888 x 1500 px. Some guidance frames the safe region as a central 4:5-ish area and warns against placing text in the lower 10-15% of the frame.

The defining Shorts risk is asymmetry. The right side is more dangerous than the left because the player places a larger vertical action column there. One current checker recommends shifting the visual center about 40 px left of the true center.

Practical Shorts placement:

- Compose faces, products, and screenshots near the center, with a slight left bias when the right side contains detail.
- Avoid relying on the right edge for important text or visual information.
- Keep subtitles and CTA text out of the bottom channel bar and music ticker.
- Use fewer, larger subtitle lines instead of dense bottom-stacked captions.

## TikTok

Use mobile-first 9:16 video at 1080 x 1920 or higher.

TikTok safe zones are highly context-dependent. Caption length, interactive add-ons, anchors, paid formats, and placement type can change how much of the frame is covered.

For organic TikTok, current working measurements in the source cluster around:

| Frame area | Working caution zone |
|---|---:|
| Top | 108-200 px |
| Bottom | 250-324 px |
| Left | ~60 px |
| Right | 120-164 px |
| Safe region examples | ~960 x 1386 px or ~900 x 1492 px |

For TikTok ads, the bottom exclusion area is stricter. Recent checkers cited in the source put the bottom margin closer to 370 px once CTA buttons such as "Shop Now" or "Learn More" appear.

Practical TikTok placement:

- Keep important text and the hero subject centered.
- Avoid the right icon rail.
- Avoid putting mission-critical information in the lower band.
- Treat the design as layered: top for identity and sound, right side for interaction, bottom for caption and commerce pressure.
- Assume an organic layout may fail once a paid CTA or interactive format is added.

## Cross-Platform Composition Rules

Center-first composition is the strongest shared pattern across Reels, Shorts, and TikTok.

For talking-head content:

- Put eyes around the upper-middle.
- Put subtitles in the lower-middle, not at the true bottom.
- Put hook text above the subtitle line rather than underneath it.

For product demos:

- Place the hero product center or slightly left of center.
- Keep price, proof points, and CTA text above the lower overlay band.
- Do not put product labels under a torso or near the lower UI stack.

For tutorials and screencasts:

- Keep key annotations near the center-left or center-top.
- Avoid the right edge and bottom strip for cursor targets, labels, steps, or key UI callouts.

The most common failure is correct aspect ratio but wrong information hierarchy: subtitles, prices, product names, subscribe prompts, or CTA copy are placed where the platform already stacks captions and controls.

## Text, Logo, and Subtitle Placement

Subtitles should live in a dedicated lower-middle band above the bottom danger zone.

Practical subtitle rule:

- Keep the lowest subtitle line at least 300-400 px above the bottom edge for most organic cross-posted work.
- Raise subtitles further for paid TikTok or Reels ads.
- Avoid more than two dense subtitle lines at once.
- Prefer fewer, larger lines over small bottom-heavy captions.

Hook text, titles, and opening copy usually work best in the upper-middle. This keeps the lower UI area clear and lets the first frame communicate before platform controls compete with the message.

Persistent logos and brand marks should not sit flush in corners. The most reliable placement is upper-left or center-left inside the safe rectangle, especially on TikTok and Shorts where the right edge is higher risk.

## Device and Preview Differences

iPhone and Android differences matter less because of raw aspect ratio and more because of notches, dynamic islands, cutouts, and app rendering. These can reduce visible headroom or shift the effective viewing area.

Do not rely only on the edit timeline. Preview in a platform-aware checker and on real devices, ideally at least one iPhone and one Android, before publishing important creative.

## Editing Workflow

Build safe zones into the project template instead of fixing them at export.

Recommended workflow:

1. Start every short-form edit on a 1080 x 1920, 9:16 canvas.
2. Add a universal 900 x 1400 centered guide for cross-platform organic safety.
3. Add a stricter ad-ready overlay with more bottom clearance for Reels and TikTok.
4. Lock the overlay or guide layer in the editor.
5. Build subtitles, hook text, product labels, logos, and CTAs inside the safe area.
6. Before final export, switch to a platform-specific overlay if the video will be published natively to one platform.
7. Preview in a checker and on real phones.

Adobe Premiere Pro safe margins are useful for general discipline, but default action-safe and title-safe margins of 10% and 20% are not enough by themselves for current vertical social platforms. Reels, Shorts, and TikTok often need asymmetric margins and larger lower exclusion zones. Use a locked PNG or guide layer for platform-specific safe zones.

In Canva, Figma, Photoshop, Final Cut, CapCut, and similar tools, the principle is the same: create or import a transparent overlay, lock it, and treat anything outside the marked boundary as at risk.

Browser editors with built-in platform safe-zone overlays can be useful when one asset is intended for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts.

## Export Checklist

Before exporting or publishing:

1. The master timeline is 1080 x 1920, 9:16.
2. The universal 900 x 1400 centered safe guide was used for cross-platform organic safety.
3. Platform-specific overlays were checked before final export.
4. Faces, products, demonstrations, and key screenshots sit inside the center band.
5. Shorts and TikTok compositions account for the riskier right-side action rail.
6. Subtitles sit in the lower-middle, not on the bottom edge.
7. Hook text and CTA copy sit in the upper-middle or center, not the Reels/TikTok lower overlay area.
8. Paid-media versions use an expanded bottom exclusion zone, especially for Meta Reels and TikTok ads.
9. Reels cover-frame behavior was checked separately for 4:5 profile-grid cropping.
10. The video was previewed in a safe-zone checker and on real devices.

The final practical standard: compose for the center, subtitle above the lower UI, and assume the bottom is unsafe until a platform-specific preview proves otherwise.
